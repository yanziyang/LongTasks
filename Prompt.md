Eiffel Tower (superpower 3)

Prompt:
/brainstorming  Here's a fully procedural 3D Eiffel Tower built in Three.js — no models, every girder generated from code using the tower's real proportions.

What makes it realistic: the silhouette follows the actual profile curve (125 m base tapering to the summit at ~300 m, antenna to 330 m), the four legs stand as independent lattice pylons that merge at the first floor, and the body is a genuine cross-braced iron lattice — thousands of X-braced panels, denser below the second floor just like the real structure. It includes the decorative double-ring arches under the first platform, all three platforms with railings at their correct heights (57 m, 115 m, 276 m), the summit cabin and broadcast mast, and the paint is graduated in three shades of "Eiffel Tower brown," darker at the base and lighter at the top, as the real tower is painted.

Drag to orbit, scroll or pinch to zoom, and it slowly rotates when idle. Toggle to Night mode for the golden illumination with the twinkling white sparkle effect from the hourly light show.

Methodology: superpowers
LLM model for planning: DeepSeek V4 Pro Max
LLM model for coding: DeepSeek V4 Flash Max
